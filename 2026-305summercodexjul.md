# Participant Information

Welcome to the **Build with Gemma Hackathon**. In this hackathon, participants will build a creative AI project using Google DeepMind’s open Gemma models.

Your goal is to create a working prototype that solves a real problem or improves an experience using Gemma. Projects can be web apps, mobile apps, Kaggle Notebooks, tools, assistants, multimodal apps, agents, or on-device AI demos.

# What You Can Build

Participants may build projects in areas such as:

- AI tools for students and learning
- Local language or accessibility tools
- Healthcare, civic engagement, or social impact apps
- Small business or productivity tools
- Multimodal apps using images, text, audio, or voice
- On-device or privacy-first AI apps
- AI agents that use tools or APIs

Gemma must be core to your project. Projects that do not use Gemma will not be eligible for judging.

---

# Submission Requirements

Each team must submit:

## 1. Kaggle Writeup

Explain your project, problem, solution, architecture, how Gemma was used, challenges, and what you built.

## 2. Working Demo

Submit a live URL, video demo, Kaggle Notebook, hosted app, terminal recording, or files judges can review.

## 3. Public Code Repository

Submit a public GitHub repo, Kaggle Notebook, or other public code link. Code should be clear enough for judges to understand how Gemma was used.

---

# Judging Criteria

Projects will be judged out of 100 points:

## Gemma Integration — 30 points

How well does the project use Gemma? Is Gemma important to the solution?

## Innovation & Impact — 30 points

Does the project solve a meaningful problem? Is the idea creative and useful?

## Functionality — 20 points

Does the prototype work? Is the demo convincing?

## Presentation & Writeup — 20 points

Is the explanation clear? Does the writeup or demo explain the problem, solution, and technical approach?

---

# Rules

- Participants must use Gemma in their project.
- Teams must submit before the deadline.
- Projects must be original work created during the hackathon.
- Participants may use open-source libraries, APIs, starter code, and AI tools, but they must explain what they used.
- Submissions must follow the hackathon code of conduct.
- Empty submissions, spam, copied projects, or projects that do not use Gemma may be disqualified.
